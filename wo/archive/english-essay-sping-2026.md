  The core message centers on how we're "defined not by the circumstances of how we exist, but by the choices we make

  genuine human connection across differences.

  The film is historically significant as the first film with a DeafBlind actor in a lead role (Robert Tarango).

 About "A-Lien" - Oscar-Nominated Short Film

  Film Details:
  - Directors: Sam and David Cutler-Kreutz
  - Year: 2023
  - Status: Nominated for Best Live Action Short Film at the 97th Academy Awards
  - Runtime: Short film format
  - IMDB Rating: 7.1/10

  Plot Summary

  The film opens with a family preparing for a scheduled green card interview. Oscar and Sophia Gomez, a married couple
  with their young daughter Nina, arrive at their appointment hoping it will grant Oscar a path to citizenship.

  The Crisis: Unexpectedly, ICE (Immigration and Customs Enforcement) agents appear at the interview location. Oscar is
  quickly apprehended and placed in a van to be deported—with Nina being taken along. Sophia rushes to their aid,
  managing to intercept them with the family's passports. Because Nina was born on American soil, she is a U.S. citizen
  and is released to her mother. However, Oscar remains in custody and is taken away.

  Core Themes

  Immigration & Family Separation
  The film explores the devastating intersection of bureaucracy and human relationships—how a system ostensibly designed
   to process immigration can suddenly become a tool for family destruction.

  Love Story & Political Commentary
  Directors describe it as "part love story, part urgent commentary on a system designed to separate rather than unite."
   The personal relationship between Oscar and Sophia provides emotional weight to the larger political critique.

  The American Dream Paradox
  The film examines the contradiction of immigration policy: Nina is born American, yet the system would still tear her
  parents apart.

  Critical Reception

  High Praise:
  - The Cinema Group: 4.5/5 stars - called it "one of the most vital and affecting nominees in the Live Action Short
  category"
  - Peter Bradshaw (The Guardian): Named it his personal favorite of the 2025 Best Live Action Short Oscar nominees,
  calling it "a film which speaks to modern America"
  - Leicestershire Press: Described it as a "visceral call to arms" regarding US immigration
  - ABC7 New York: Highlighted how the film "delves into the US immigration process" and shows "what happens when ICE
  agents appear"

  Filmmaker Vision

  Directors Sam and David Cutler-Kreutz crafted this as an urgent contemporary commentary. The film doesn't present
  immigration as an abstract policy debate—it grounds it in the lived experience of one family in a single, harrowing
  moment.
