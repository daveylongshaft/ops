Explore a Living Primate aka "Adopt a Primate"



find information about a living Orangutan. Research them, enough for 3 pages. Wikipedia and the like is not good enough. Any web page belonging to a physical anthropologist, primatologist, or biologist is. Things to learn about your species and put in the report: their common and scientific names, where they live (with map), habitat, numbers (how many of 'em are there), what they look like, perhaps the parts of their bodies we can't see, what they eat, what eats them, other dangers to them that exist, their behavior, especially social behavior (which by definition includes reproductive behavior but I want to make that clear by being redundant), and their endangered status. You can add other tidbits if you like. You can certainly add some of your own thoughts about the species if you like, including describing your emotional reaction to them, that they are my favorite primate, what you learned that was surprising about them, and so forth. You also need at least two photos or videos of them. Videos you took on your phone at the zoo certainly count!



Put all that into paragraphs. Write for an 8th grade reading and comprehension level.  Use the spell checker and grammar tool if you have one. At least 2 pages of text, and you can certainly get an A with no more than 3 pages of text. Make sure you include the map and 2+ photos/videos. Include your sources, including for the map and video. A link to where you found them is fine. If you are stuck on how to organize things, go basic description, behavior/eating/being eaten, your response/reflection.
